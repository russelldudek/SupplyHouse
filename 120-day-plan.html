Shared direction

Enterprise adoption thesis linked to business objectives, employee experience and decision rights.

Visible portfolio

Proven mechanism

Two bounded workflows commissioned with explicit authority, enablement and evidence.

Installed capability

Operating principles

PrincipleBehavior
Listen before prescriptionWorkflow observation, employee signal and prior-change learning precede tool selection.
Business-owned workEvery use case has a functional owner accountable for the outcome and adoption.
Human authority by designApproval, override, escalation and support are explicit before launch.
Evidence before expansionScale follows quality, sustained use, confidence, access and business evidence.
Capability over dependenceStandards and enablement help teams make better decisions rather than wait for a central group.

First-week listening agenda

Executive and functional leaders

  • Clarify expectations, decision rights and success narrative.
  • Ask for one valuable workflow, one feared failure and one change employees adopted well.
  • Understand current investment, technical commitments and governance.

Employees and managers

  • Observe current systems, handoffs, workarounds and informal support.
  • Listen for rational skepticism, unequal access and career concerns.
  • Identify trusted informal teachers and existing local experiments.

Questions the first 30 days must answer

Value and workflow

Which workflows constrain growth, customer service, margin, capacity or employee experience? Where is value already visible?

Technology and risk

What tools, data, vendors, support paths, legal boundaries and security requirements already exist?

People and adoption

Where are curiosity, fear, confidence, access and change fatigue? Who teaches others?

Evidence and economics

How are usage, quality, risk, value and employee confidence currently measured?

Day-30 executive decision

Agree on two bounded workflows to commission, the owners and evidence required, the support model, and what will explicitly remain out of scope during the first 120 days.

Pilot selection guardrails

Credible value

The workflow matters enough to justify change and has a visible comparison baseline.

Engaged ownership

A functional leader and employee users will co-design, review evidence and own the outcome.

Bounded learning

The pilot can test a narrow AI role, explicit authority and a reversible path within the first 90 days.

Days 61–90 · Operate and learn

Weekly pilot review

Business owner, IT/Data, risk partners and adoption lead review evidence, failure modes and decisions.

Visible support

Office hours, manager reinforcement, quick reference, example library and escalation path.

Learning discipline

Log corrections, overrides, abandonment, recurring questions, workarounds and reusable components.

Illustrative pilot scorecard

QuestionEvidence categoryDecision use
Is the workflow better?Cycle time, rework, backlog, service, capacity, decision qualityValidate or reject the value hypothesis
Is use appropriate?Evaluation, correction, override, escalation, auditabilityTighten AI role, context or human checkpoints
Are people adopting it?Eligible use, depth, abandonment, workarounds, support demandRepair workflow fit or enablement
Do people feel capable and safe?Confidence, clarity of limits, access to help, equitable accessImprove training, communication and support
Can the pattern travel?Reusable components, stable data, owner, known failure modesScale, repair, hold or stop

Day-90 disposition

ScaleEvidence supports wider use with a named owner.
RepairValue remains but fit, controls or enablement need redesign.
HoldReadiness or evidence is insufficient for the next step.
Stop / human-ledThe work does not justify additional AI involvement.

Communication cadence

Executive sponsor update every two weeks; functional-owner decision review weekly; office hours weekly; ambassador learning circle biweekly; concise employee communication tied to real work and explicit limits; Board-level reporting only after evidence definitions are stable.

Executive portfolio scorecard

Outcome

Workflow value, customer or employee consequence and economic evidence.

Trust and control

Quality, exceptions, auditability, human authority and risk posture.

Day-120 executive review

  • What changed in the work, and what evidence remains incomplete?
  • Where did employee skepticism expose a real design problem?
  • Which components, controls and enablement patterns are reusable?
  • Which use cases should enter the next fit review?
  • What team shape, vendor posture and investment are justified by evidence?
  • What should remain explicitly out of scope?

12-month horizon

Desired state: AI adoption is no longer a campaign or collection of tools. It is a visible operating discipline: business-owned, technically supported, responsibly governed, role-enabled and measured by work outcomes plus employee confidence.
