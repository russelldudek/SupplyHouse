Why this role matters now

SupplyHouse has already proved AI can improve a critical operating system.

The opportunity now is larger than another successful tool deployment. It is to turn isolated wins into a repeatable enterprise capability—without weakening the ownership, service and people-first culture that made the company successful.

Public evidence: SupplyHouse and KKR announced a 2025 strategic partnership to support long-term growth; RELEX reports SupplyHouse deployed AI-driven forecasting and replenishment across four fulfillment centers, reducing manual planning effort and improving supply-chain outcomes. These facts establish context, not undisclosed internal conditions.

The operating tension

Growth makes AI more valuable—and careless adoption more expensive.

A centralized program can create standards but miss the work. Local experimentation can find value but create sprawl. The role succeeds by making the fit between workflow, people, controls and proof visible before scale.

What the current pattern protects

Local judgment, employee trust, practical expertise, customer responsiveness and the freedom to solve real problems.

What unresolved fragmentation costs

Duplicated experiments, uneven access, unclear authority, weak reuse and difficulty distinguishing a promising demo from a durable operating pattern.

Speed / trustMove quickly enough to matter without outrunning confidence or control.
Enterprise / localShare standards and platforms while keeping workflow ownership near the work.
Automation / agencyUse AI where it helps while making human authority unmistakable.

The operating model

Install capability—not dependence.

The adoption function should make business teams more capable of choosing, using and improving AI, while keeping enterprise standards visible and supportable.

01

Read the work

Observe the workflow, exceptions, informal knowledge, friction and employee concern before selecting technology.

02

Fit the tool

Define the AI role, source context, human authority, support owner and controls as one design—not separate workstreams.

03

Prove the gain

Measure workflow outcome, quality, appropriate reliance, adoption depth, confidence and access—not logins or demos.

04

Install the capability

Turn proven learning into reusable patterns, role-based enablement, ambassador support, governance and investment decisions.

First 120 days

Earn context. Prove the mechanism. Leave a system behind.

Install the capability

Questions I would use to listen

The first strategy is better questions.

These are hypotheses for discovery, not claims about SupplyHouse's internal state.

01

Where has AI already made work meaningfully better—and what made people trust that change?

02

Which workflow is creating the most repeated friction across teams, handoffs or customer commitments?

03

What are employees most skeptical about, and what might that skepticism be protecting?

04

Which decisions should be enterprise standards, and which should stay with business owners closest to the work?

05

What evidence would make the executive team comfortable scaling an AI pattern—and equally comfortable stopping one?
