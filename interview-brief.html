Independent candidate work

Fit AI to the work. Bring the people with it.

A source-grounded point of view for enterprise AI adoption at SupplyHouse

Executive thesis

SupplyHouse should treat enterprise AI adoption as a fit-and-install discipline. Start with a valuable workflow. Define the data and context it can trust. Bound the AI role. Make human authority visible. Prove the result in the work. Then package the learning so the next team starts further ahead.

Why now

Growth pressure

SupplyHouse and KKR announced a 2025 strategic partnership to support long-term growth while preserving culture.

Proven AI value

RELEX reports AI-driven forecasting and replenishment across four fulfillment centers reduced manual planning effort and improved supply-chain outcomes.

People-first objective

The role explicitly prioritizes listening, psychological safety, equitable access, employee confidence and skepticism as signal.

The actual work

Turn distributed AI demand and point successes into a governed portfolio of adopted workflows that produce measurable business value, stronger employee capability and reusable enterprise learning.

Operating tensions

Cost of leaving the tension unresolved

Not catastrophe—operating drag: repeated experiments, inconsistent support, unclear ownership, uneven access, weak confidence and an inability to tell which local success should become an enterprise pattern.

Stakeholder topology

Outcome owners

Procurement, Operations, Sales, Customer Service and Finance leaders accountable for workflows and value.

Capability partners

IT, Data, Security, HR, Legal, Finance, vendors, managers and employee ambassadors.

People affected

Frontline employees, managers, support teams and customers whose work or experience changes.

Decisions this role should make easier

What work changes; which opportunities receive funding and technical capacity; what becomes a shared standard; where human judgment remains final; what escalates; what is repaired; and what is stopped.

One standard for deciding what deserves an install.

Qualitative conditions—not an invented maturity score

The five fit conditions

1. Job signal

A real workflow has observable friction, consequence and an accountable business owner.

2. Context + data

The sources, definitions, freshness, permissions and failure modes are understood well enough for the intended use.

3. AI role

Assist, analyze, recommend or act is explicit. The model does not quietly acquire decision authority.

4. Human authority

Approval, override, escalation, support and accountability are named before launch.

5. Proof + reuse

Workflow outcome, quality, appropriate reliance, adoption depth, confidence and access are measured.

Decision

Commission a bounded pilot, run discovery, repair the design, hold for readiness or keep the work human-led.

Balanced scorecard

Business

Cycle time, capacity, rework, backlog, service, margin or decision quality—selected per workflow.

Trust + quality

Evaluation results, correction, override, escalation, auditability and appropriate reliance.

Adoption + capability

Eligible use, depth, abandonment, confidence, equitable access, support demand and reusable components.

Center of Excellence design

A small enabling core—not a central gatekeeping bureaucracy. It provides fit standards, portfolio visibility, vendor and technical partnership, responsible-use patterns, role-based enablement, evaluation support, ambassador infrastructure and executive scorecards. Business owners remain accountable for the workflow and outcome.

The credible hiring question

Can an operator-technologist lead enterprise-wide AI adoption? My evidence says the integrated nature of the work is the point: I have repeatedly connected technology, workflow, people, controls, customer consequence and operating evidence. I would not pretend to know SupplyHouse's internal portfolio on day one. I would bring a disciplined way to learn it quickly, make decisions visible and build capability with the people doing the work.

First-120-day outcome

Context

Verified portfolio, stakeholder, readiness and adoption map.

Proof

Two bounded workflows with scale, repair, hold or stop decisions.

Capability

Practical CoE, ambassador rhythm, enablement and governance patterns.

Direction

Second-wave portfolio and evidence-based investment narrative.

Executive questions

  • Where has AI already improved work, and what made employees trust that change?
  • Which workflows constrain growth, service, margin or capacity across more than one function?
  • What skepticism is the organization hearing, and what might it be protecting?
  • Which decisions should be enterprise standards, and which should remain local?
  • What evidence would make the executive team comfortable scaling—or stopping—an AI pattern?

Closing position

The goal is not to maximize AI deployed. It is to make the organization better at choosing, installing, governing and improving AI-enabled work—while increasing employee capability and confidence.
