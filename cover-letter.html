Fit AI to the work. Bring the people with it.

July 22, 2026 · Hiring Team, SupplyHouse

Dear SupplyHouse Hiring Team,

SupplyHouse is hiring for more than an AI strategy. The role description points to a harder operating outcome: make AI useful across functions, make employees confident enough to use it responsibly, and give leaders evidence that distinguishes a scalable pattern from an isolated win.

Public evidence makes the timing especially compelling. SupplyHouse has already used AI-driven forecasting and replenishment across four fulfillment centers, while the company's strategic partnership with KKR is intended to support long-term growth. The adoption challenge is now to help successful local work travel across functions without losing the culture, judgment and customer focus that people are protecting.

That combination—work, technology, people, controls and proof—is the through-line of my career. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, purchasing, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack and machine telemetry. The work is not a software rollout. It is making signals visible, redesigning handoffs, creating standard work and training, clarifying ownership, and helping teams use new capability under real operating pressure.

Through DudeWorth, I design AI-readiness and agentic workflow patterns that connect business value, data context, human judgment, escalation, evaluation and governance. At Amazon, I built operating mechanisms in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million annual second-day orders. At Compunetics, I connected engineering, manufacturing, quality and customer delivery in high-reliability technical work. Together, those experiences give me a practical perspective on enterprise adoption: standards matter, but the change only becomes real inside a workflow owned by people.

My candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/SupplyHouse/ makes that thinking visible. It proposes an AI Fit-to-Work Standard, a first-120-day entry plan and a one-page review mechanism for deciding whether a workflow should be piloted, repaired, held or kept human-led. The model is intentionally a starting hypothesis; the first work would be listening to SupplyHouse leaders and employees, observing how work is actually done, and learning what prior change efforts earned trust.

I would bring a people-first, evidence-disciplined approach to building the roadmap, Center of Excellence, ambassador network, role-based enablement, governance and measurement system described in the role. My aim would be straightforward: help SupplyHouse choose the right AI for the right job, preserve clear human authority, and turn each proven implementation into reusable capability for the next team.

In the first 120 days, I would replace assumptions with an operating picture of current use, workflow friction, employee signal, technical boundaries and decision rights. From that evidence, I would commission two bounded workflows with engaged business owners, operate them with visible support and weekly decision reviews, and convert the learning into a practical CoE, ambassador rhythm, scorecard and second-wave portfolio. A stopped pilot would be useful learning; an unsupported rollout would not.

AI tools assisted research, layout and production of my application materials. The strategy, evidence choices, operating model and interview judgment are my own.

Carpe diem,
