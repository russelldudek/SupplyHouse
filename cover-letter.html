Dear SupplyHouse Hiring Team,

The hardest part of enterprise AI is rarely selecting a capable tool. It is creating the conditions for people to use it confidently, leaders to trust it, and the business to know whether it is actually improving the work. Your posting is unusually direct about that reality: culture, adoption and psychological safety are not supporting activities; they are the work.

SupplyHouse also appears to have a meaningful starting point. Public evidence shows that AI-enabled forecasting and replenishment already reduced manual effort and helped demand planners focus on more strategic work. The opportunity now is to turn point success into an enterprise capability across procurement, operations, sales, customer service and finance—without losing the people-first culture that made the company successful.

That is the boundary where I have spent my career. At Amazon, I led frontline operations and launch work at high scale using Lean, Gemba, standard work and visible operating routines. At Compunetics, I connected engineering, manufacturing, quality and customer requirements in high-reliability technical environments. At Cardinal, I modernized distributed commercial and logistics workflows. Today at Vape-Jet, I am leading an AI-first operating transformation across production, purchasing, quality, customer support, engineering issue flow, ERP, CRM and machine telemetry. Through DudeWorth, I have built AI-readiness and agentic-workflow frameworks that connect value, data, human authority, governance, adoption and evidence.

My thesis for SupplyHouse is simple: make AI useful enough to trust—and trusted enough to scale. I would build a disciplined adoption system in which every use case begins inside real work, has a named business owner, retains explicit human authority, includes role-based enablement and earns scale through measured outcomes and employee confidence.

I made that thinking visible in the accompanying candidate vision package. It includes an AI Adoption Manifold, a workflow commissioning review, an interview thesis brief and a first-120-day plan. It is independent work based only on public information and my verified career evidence.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where SupplyHouse has already earned AI trust, where adoption is stalling, and what a measurable enterprise capability should look like twelve months from now.

Carpe diem,

Russell Dudek
